#map vs #each

10/05/2014

Let's look at something nice and simple this week--#each vs #map!. Just picture me as Bob Ross, showing you a couple of happy little methods.

It can be kind of tricky--because #each and #map sound like they're doing the same thing--here's the definition of #each: "Calls the given block once for each element in self, passing that element as a parameter."

And here's the #map definition: "Invokes the given block once for each element of self. Creates a new array containing the values returned by the block."

Let's talk about what they BOTH do first--they both cycle through the elements of the array and do whatever you asked them to do in the block. It's pretty darn useful.

But here's where things change--#each isn't really permanent--it's great for styling your output, making things print out a certain way. BUT--when you want to PERMANENTLY CHANGE an array--you use #map!. And when you want to create a new array, it's best to use #map and set that equal to the new variable holding the new array.

Why is that? Because #each isn't going to handle math computations--it'll just give you back the array. But #map will!
